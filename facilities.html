Portable Tribometer

A portable tribometer for rail applications is a compact, mobile device designed to measure the friction coefficient between train wheels and rails in the field.

Measures the coefficient of friction between the rail head surface and a testing wheel. Can be placed on a rail and set different lateral position.

Speed of the wheel can be set up to 0.4 m/s. The slip range is controlled by the braking torque from 0 to 100% slip.

Results can be directly transferred to computer. Measured data are then fitted by any of the standard models for traciton curve.

Small scale TwinDisc

A small-scale twin-disc machine is a precision laboratory device used to simulate and study rolling-sliding contact between two surfaces under controlled conditions.

It is used for investigating tribological behavior, including friction, wear, and material interactions. Measures the coefficient of friction between two discs with diameter of 40 mm and width 10 mm.

Speed of the specimens can be set up to 2 m/s. The slip range is controlled by the driving motors from 0 to 100% slip. The maximum contact pressure can be set up to 1.2 GPa.

Features: Due to the size, the contact specimen can be cut from real wheel and rail material.

Mini Traction Machine

The Mini Traction Machine, developed by PCS Instruments, is a precision bench-top tribometer used to measure friction and traction behavior.

It is primarily used to evaluate the performance of lubricants, coatings, and materials.

The device can generate contact pressures up to about 1.25 GPa. It operates up to speeds of 4 m/s and can be run under controlled temperature environments.

The measured lubricant can be heated to higher temperatures. It also includes an enviromenmtal chamber controlling the surrounding temperature and humidity.

Large scale TwinDisc

This large-scale twin-disc machine is a specialized tribological test rig designed to replicate the contact mechanics between a full-size railway wheel and rail.

This full-scale device is used to measure the conditions of wheelset negotiating a curve by setting the angle of attack, enabeling the measurement of lateral forces as well as the noise generated by contact.

It measures the lateral and tangential forces of the specimen with a set angle of attack. The speed can be set up to 3 m/s with contact pressure as high as 900 MPa.

This device enables the measurement of noise reduction properties of wheel-rail lubricants. Application devices for lubricants can be moutned to the frame to simulate applicaiton of friciton managing products.

High Pressure Torsion rheometer

This device is a precision tribological instrument designed to measure the elastoplastic properties of materials and applied products under pure shear loading.

This rheometer enables the measurement of basic shear response of the contact interface. The results can be used in simualtions to predict fricitonal behavior.

High Pressure Torsion rheometer can create contact pressure up to 1 GPa with realistic wheel-rail contact size. It can precisely measure the shear stress in contact interface under slow loading of 1 µm/s.
