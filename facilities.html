Portable Tribometer

A portable tribometer for rail applications is a compact, mobile device designed to measure the friction coefficient between train wheels and rails in the field. These devices are crucial for ensuring safety, optimizing braking performance, and maintaining rail infrastructure.

Purpose and Application: Measures the coefficient of friction between the rail head surface and a testing wheel. Can be placed on a rail and set different lateral position.

Measurement Capabilities: Speed of the wheel can be set up to 0.4 m/s. The slip range is controlled by the braking torque from 0 to 100% slip. Can measure minimum coefficient of friction of 0.04.

Features: Results can be directly transferred to computer. Measured data are then fitted by any of the standard models for traciton curve.

Small scale TwinDisc

A small-scale twin-disc machine is a precision laboratory device used to simulate and study rolling-sliding contact between two surfaces under controlled conditions. It is widely used for investigating tribological behavior, including friction, wear, and material interactions.

Purpose and Application: It is widely used for investigating tribological behavior, including friction, wear, and material interactions. Measures the coefficient of friction between two discs with diameter of 40 mm and width 10 mm.

Measurement Capabilities: Speed of the specimens can be set up to 2 m/s. The slip range is controlled by the driving motors from 0 to 100% slip. The maximum contact pressure can be set up to 1.2 GPa.

Features: Due to the size, the contact specimen can be cut from real wheel and rail material.

Mini Traction Machine

The Mini Traction Machine, developed by PCS Instruments, is a precision bench-top tribometer used to measure friction and traction behavior between lubricated or unlubricated contacting surfaces under a wide range of controlled conditions.

Purpose and Application: It is primarily used to evaluate the performance of lubricants, coatings, and materials under boundary and mixed lubrication regimes.

Measurement Capabilities: It measures the coefficient of friction in real time by monitoring the tangential and normal forces between a rotating ball and disc. It also records key parameters such as speed, load, temperature, and slide-to-roll ratio, allowing detailed analysis of lubrication regimes and surface interactions.

Features: The measured lubricant can be heated to higher temperatures. It also includes an enviromenmtal chamber controlling the surrounding temperature and humidity.

Large scale TwinDisc

This large-scale twin-disc machine is a specialized tribological test rig designed to replicate the contact mechanics between a full-size railway wheel and rail, where the wheel geometry is represented at actual size, while the rail is simulated by a smaller scaled down disc.

Purpose and Application: This full-scale device is used to measure the conditions of wheelset negotiating a curve by setting the angle of attack. This enables the measurement of lateral forces as well as the noise generated by contact.

Measurement Capabilities: It measures the lateral and tangential forces of the specimen with a set angle of attack. The speed can be set up to 3 m/s with contact pressure as high as 900 MPa.

Features: This device enables the measurement of noise reduction properties of wheel-rail lubricants. Application devices for lubricants can be moutned to the frame to simulate applicaiton of friciton managing products.

High Pressure Torsion rheometer

This device is a precision tribological instrument designed to measure the elastoplastic properties of materials and applied productsunder pure shear loading. It operates by applying very small, controlled torsional displacements (on the order of micrometers per second) between contacting surfaces under high normal pressures, simulating real-world load conditions in wheel-rail contact.

Purpose and Application: This rheometer enables the measurement of basic shear response of the contact interface. The results can be used in simualtions to predict fricitonal behavior.

Measurement Capabilities: High Pressure Torsion rheometer can create contact pressure up to 1 GPa. This is in realistic contact area of wheel-rail contact. It can precisely measure the shear stress in contact interface under slow loading of 1 µm/s.

Features: Measurements of base materials as well as applied products can be donu under increased temperature up to 100 °C.
