What do we do ?

At our research group, we explore the complexities of wheel-rail contact, addressing key challenges such as friction, lubrication, and acoustics. We conduct both fundamental and applied research, using a combination of simulations and experimental work in our laboratory, equipped with devices of various scales. Our team also develops new technologies and systems to improve the performance and reliability of rail infrastructure.

Friction

Friction measurements in laboratory or field.
  • Lubricants and friction modifiers
  • Laboratory and track measurements
  • Surface contamination

Noise

Evaluation of noise levels generated by contact.
  • Systems for noise reduction
  • Track application units
  • Causes of noise emissions

Simulation

Numerical predictions of friction and lubrication algorithms.
  • Onboard predictive systems
  • Digital twin models
  • Contact mechanics

Motivation and social relevance

Who are we ?

We are a dedicated research group based at Brno University of Technology, specializing in the study of wheel-rail contact. Our work spans a range of critical areas, including friction analysis, lubrication systems, acoustics, and advanced simulations. In our state-of-the-art laboratory, we conduct experiments using devices of various scales to better understand the complex dynamics of wheel-rail interactions. By combining theoretical research with hands-on testing, we aim to provide innovative solutions to real-world challenges. Collaboration is at the heart of what we do. We work closely with industrial partners and other research institutions, ensuring our findings are both cutting-edge and practical for industry applications.

People

Our team consists of dedicated individuals ranging from bachelor's to PhD students, research assistants and associate proffesors.

Goals

Our primary goal is to advance the understanding of wheel-rail contact through cutting-edge research and innovative solutions.

Vision

Our vision is to be at the forefront of innovation in the field of wheel-rail contact, shaping the future of railway systems worldwide.
