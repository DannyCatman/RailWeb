Friction

Our goal is to improve the understanding and performance of friction management solutions for rail applications through both laboratory and field-based testing. Our team utilizes both commercial and custom-built tribological testing devices tailored to a wide range of applications—from portable field instruments to high-precision lab-based tribometers.

Noise

We conduct both laboratory and field investigations focused on noise generated by the wheel–rail contact. In the lab, we measure noise emissions to study the mechanisms of sound generation and assess the impact of applied lubricants on noise reduction. In the field, we deploy stationary acoustic measurement units, particularly in urban transport settings such as tram networks, to monitor real-world noise levels and evaluate the effectiveness of friction management products.

Monitoring

Long-term monitoring of noise emissions in urban areas

Development of stationary measuring units

Mitigation

Evaluation of lubricants to supress noise emission

Simulation

We perform advanced simulations to analyze the frictional behavior of the wheel–rail contact, including the redistribution and effectiveness of applied lubricants. Our work also involves the design and optimization of lubrication systems for both trams and trains. In addition to friction modeling, we simulate noise generation at the contact interface and conduct finite element method (FEM) simulations to study both structural dynamics and acoustic responses.
