Friction

Our team utilizes both commercial and custom-built tribological testing devices tailored to a wide range of applications—from portable field instruments to high-precision lab-based tribometers that ensure reproducible results across laboratories worldwide. Our custom-built devices are specifically designed to operate under a broad range of conditions, including extreme loads, speeds, and environmental factors, and are also developed for use in real-world field measurements. Our primary focus is on investigating friction management products for railhead and wheel flange contact, and we are actively working to develop standardized, step-by-step testing protocols to reliably assess and compare product performance.

Goals

Our goal is to improve the understanding and performance of friction management solutions for rail applications through both laboratory and field-based testing.

Applied research

Close cooperation with industry partners in the field of lubrication systems for rail vehicles enables us to develop cutting-edge solutions for commercial use. We try to improve not only the eprformance of applied lubricants, but also find new and better solutions for applications systems moutned to the vehicle.

Fundamental research

We study fundamental properties of lubricants as well as design new lubricant compositions for both liquid and solid products. Frictional measurements are done on different laboratory scales as well as field measurement. This enables us to understand how simplified measurement can be carried over to field conditions.

Noise

We conduct both laboratory and field investigations focused on noise generated by the wheel–rail contact. In the lab, we measure noise emissions under controlled conditions to study the mechanisms of sound generation and assess the impact of applied lubricants on noise reduction. In the field, we deploy stationary acoustic measurement units, particularly in urban transport settings such as tram networks, to monitor real-world noise levels and evaluate the effectiveness of friction management products.

Goals

Our goal is to understand and reduce noise generated by wheel–rail interactions through comprehensive laboratory and field measurements combined with advanced simulations. We aim to develop and optimize friction management and lubrication strategies that minimize noise emissions, improving the comfort and environmental impact of rail and tram transport systems.

Applied research

Cooperation with Brno Public Transport company gives us unique opportunities to investigate the development of noise year around. Such informations are crucial to understand how and when is noise generated. In our previous investigations, we were able to identify specific vehicles that caused excessive noise during curving.

Fundamental research

The use of state-of-the art acoustic measurements in combination with numerical models helps us understand the causes of noise generation. We combine this approach with friciton management solutions to find the best practices in application to urban area for reduction of noise levels.

Simulaiton

We perform advanced simulations to analyze the frictional behavior of the wheel–rail contact, including the redistribution and effectiveness of applied lubricants. Our work also involves the design and optimization of lubrication systems for both trams and trains. In addition to friction modeling, we simulate noise generation at the contact interface and conduct finite element method (FEM) simulations to study both structural dynamics and acoustic responses. These tools help us better understand real-world performance and support the development of effective friction management solutions.

Goals

We want to leverage advanced simulations to accurately predict frictional behavior, lubricant performance, and noise generation in wheel–rail contact. By integrating structural and acoustic modeling, we aim to optimize lubrication system designs and develop effective noise mitigation strategies that enhance the safety, efficiency, and sustainability of rail and tram operations.

Applied research

Our primary goal is to advance the understanding of wheel-rail contact through cutting-edge research and innovative solutions.

Fundamental research

Our vision is to be at the forefront of innovation in the field of wheel-rail contact, shaping the future of railway systems worldwide. We aspire to lead groundbreaking research that not only advances academic understanding but also drives practical advancements in industry.
